Chickpea curry recipe

Chickpea curry is a popular, nutritious, and budget-friendly vegetarian dish featuring chickpeas simmered in a spiced sauce, commonly made with onions, tomatoes, and coconut milk or yogurt. Known for its versatility, this one-pot meal often incorporates Indian spices like garam masala, turmeric, cumin, and ginger, providing a savory, creamy, and mildly spicy flavor.

A basic chickpea curry needs aromatics (onion, garlic, ginger), spices (cumin, coriander, curry powder, turmeric, garam masala), a liquid base (coconut milk, crushed tomatoes, or broth), canned chickpeas, oil, and salt, often finished with fresh cilantro and lime juice, with optional additions like spinach or chili for heat.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons fresh ginger root, finely chopped
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 2 (2-inch) sticks cinnamon, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, with liquid
  • 1 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Heat oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until onions are tender, about 3 to 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in garlic, ginger, cloves, cinnamon, cumin, coriander, salt, cayenne, and turmeric. Cook for 1 minute over medium heat, stirring constantly.
  4. Mix in chickpeas and their liquid. Continue to cook and stir until heated through. Remove from heat. Stir in cilantro just before serving, reserving 1 tablespoon for garnish.
  5. Enjoy!
